About Football

Football, also known as soccer, is a global sport played between two teams of 11 players.

Played on a rectangular field with a goal at each end, the aim is to score more goals than the opponent by getting the ball into the net.

It’s governed by the Laws of the Game, with major tournaments like the FIFA World Cup and UEFA Champions League bringing nations and clubs together.

Key Rules
  • 11 Players per team
  • 90 minutes (2 halves)
  • Score by getting the ball into the net
  • Offside: no sneaky positioning
  • No tripping or handballs (except goalie)
Top Leagues
  • EPL (England)
  • La Liga (Spain)
  • Serie A (Italy)
  • Bundesliga (Germany)
  • Ligue 1 (France)
  • MLS (USA/Canada)
  • Brasileirão (Brazil)
  • Argentine Primera
  • Saudi Pro League
  • Eredivisie (Netherlands)
Governing Body

FIFA is the global headquarters of football, managing international tournaments and enforcing the Laws of the Game. It's where global decisions are made.
